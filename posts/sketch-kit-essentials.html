Building a Beginner's Sketch Kit

17 Jun 26 · 5 min read

You do not need a studio full of supplies to start sketching. A small kit that travels with you will get more use than expensive tools that stay in a drawer. Here is what to put in it.

Sketchbook paper

Look for paper labelled sketching or drawing around 100–130 gsm. It should take erasing without tearing and feel slightly textured (called tooth) so graphite grips the surface.

  • Spiral-bound — lies flat on a table or your knee.
  • A5 or 5 × 8 in — portable without feeling cramped.
  • Perforated pages — optional, but handy if you want to tear out studies.

Pencils

Graphite pencils are graded from hard (H) to soft (B). For sketching, three pencils are enough:

  • HB — everyday lines and light shading.
  • 2B — darker marks with less pressure, ideal for quick studies.
  • 4B or 6B — deep shadows and expressive drawing.

Mechanical pencils work too — use 0.5 mm or 0.7 mm lead around HB hardness for consistent line weight.

Erasers and extras

A kneaded eraser lifts graphite gently and can be shaped to a point for highlights. A standard vinyl eraser handles clean corrections. Add a small pencil sharpener or craft knife, and optionally a blending stump for smooth shading.

Under-bag budget kit One A5 sketchbook, an HB and 2B pencil, a kneaded eraser, and a pen for ink notes. That is a complete kit — everything else is a nice-to-have.

Pen sketching (optional)

Many urban sketchers work entirely in ink. A waterproof fineliner (0.3–0.5 mm) or a simple ballpoint forces you to commit to lines and builds confidence fast. Pair it with a waterbrush and a small watercolour set if you want to add colour later.

Buy simple, use it often, and upgrade individual items only when you know what you are missing. The best sketch kit is the one that is already in your bag.
