5 Watercolour Supplies Every Beginner Needs

June 2026 · 5 min read

Starting watercolour can feel overwhelming — there are hundreds of paints, papers, and brushes to choose from. Here is a lean starter kit that covers the essentials without breaking the bank.

1. A 12-pan watercolour set

Look for a student-grade set with a warm and cool version of each primary colour (red, yellow, blue). Brands like Winsor & Newton Cotman or Sakura Koi offer good pigment at a fair price. Avoid sets with 48 colours — you will rarely use half of them.

2. Cold-press watercolour paper

Paper matters more than paint when you are learning. Cold-press (sometimes labelled "NOT") has a slight texture that forgives mistakes and holds water well. Start with 300 gsm (140 lb) pads from Canson XL or Strathmore.

3. Three round brushes

You only need sizes 6, 10, and a fine detail brush (size 2 or 0). Synthetic sable blends are affordable and hold a point nicely. One flat brush for washes is a bonus, not a necessity.

4. Two jars of water

Yes, this counts as a supply. One jar for rinsing, one for clean water. Dirty water muddies your colours faster than anything else.

5. Masking tape and a board

Tape your paper to a stiff board or clipboard. It keeps the sheet flat as it dries and gives you a clean white border — a small thing that makes your work feel finished.

Pro tip Spend your budget on paper first, then brushes, then paints. Good paper makes average paints look great; cheap paper makes everything frustrating.

That is the whole kit. Add a pencil, a kneaded eraser, and a kitchen roll for blotting, and you are ready to paint.
